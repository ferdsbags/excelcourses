The Artifact Sequence Approach

The Artifact Sequence Approach is a structured instructional methodology that organizes learning around the creation and progression of tangible, functional outputs called artifacts. Rather than teaching isolated skills in random order, each phase produces a concrete deliverable that serves as both the product of learning and the foundation for the next phase.

In Advanced Excel, each artifact is a working component of a larger analytical system: a structured data foundation, a controlled calculation layer, a decision-ready output, or a validated model prepared for scale. Participants build each one in sequence, under instructor guidance, with no phase skipped and no artifact left incomplete before the next begins.

In each phase, learning follows a two-step pattern. The instructor first demonstrates the build live, walking through every decision as the artifact takes shape. Participants then reproduce the same process independently, applying what was just observed to produce their own working output. This follow-along then individual activity structure ensures that every participant completes each phase with a verified, functional artifact before the next phase begins.

The completed artifact serves as the measure of learning. A well-built model holds up when data grows, stays accurate when assumptions are updated, and remains clear to anyone who opens it. Reaching that standard requires working at the higher cognitive levels that advanced Excel demands: Analyze, Evaluate, Create.

Each course is stand-alone. Participants arrive with their existing knowledge and leave with a fully built, instructor-verified system they can reproduce on their own data, for their own business questions, from the following week.

1

Define the Analytical Objective

Clarify the decision context and reporting requirement. Identify required outputs and define how success will be evaluated. Establish assumptions and constraints that will govern the build.

2

Establish Structured Foundations

Organize raw data into controlled tables. Separate input and assumption areas from calculated areas. Standardize formats and define naming conventions to ensure structural integrity before calculations begin.

3

Design and Implement Processing Logic

Plan the system architecture and dependency flow. Apply aggregation, lookup, transformation, or automation logic. Control calculation behavior and prevent hard-coded or circular references.

4

Generate Decision-Ready Outputs

Produce interpretable results aligned to the original objective. Apply clarity, hierarchy, and structured presentation standards so outputs communicate insight to the intended audience.

5

Validate, Stress-Test, and Prepare for Scale

Audit formula chains and logic integrity. Test behavior under expanded datasets and extreme inputs. Confirm recalculation accuracy and prepare the model for recurring or enterprise use.
